# 2024 Rules Glossary Phrasing Reference

A quick reference for commonly needed phrasing conventions from the 2024 Player's Handbook and Dungeon Master's Guide. When in doubt, cross-reference against published 2024 content.

---

## Conditions

Always capitalize condition names in mechanical text. Preferred phrasing:

- "the target has the Prone condition" (not "the target is prone")
- "you gain the Invisible condition" (not "you become invisible")
- "while you have the Grappled condition" (not "while grappled")

Full condition list: Blinded, Charmed, Deafened, Exhaustion, Frightened, Grappled, Incapacitated, Invisible, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained, Stunned, Unconscious

Note: Exhaustion is measured in levels. "You gain 1 Exhaustion level" is the correct phrasing.

---

## Action Economy

- "You take the Attack action" / "you can take the Attack action"
- Action types: Action, Bonus Action, Reaction, Free Object Interaction
- "as part of the Attack action" (not "as part of attacking")
- "you can use your Reaction" (not "spend your reaction")
- Opportunity Attacks: "make an Opportunity Attack" (capitalized in 2024)

---

## Saving Throws and Checks

- "make a Dexterity saving throw" (not "roll a Dexterity saving throw")
- "make a DC [X] Strength saving throw"
- "make a Wisdom (Perception) check" — ability first, skill in parentheses
- "the DC equals [formula]" (not "the difficulty class is")

---

## Hit Points and Healing

- "regain [X] hit points" (not "restore" or "recover" or "heal for")
- "you drop to 0 hit points" (not "you're reduced to 0 hit points" in most cases — check context)
- "a creature within range regains [X] hit points"
- "your hit point maximum"

---

## Spell Slots and Casting

- "expend a spell slot" (not "use" or "spend")
- "expend a spell slot of [X] level or higher"
- "cast this spell using a higher-level spell slot"
- "you regain all expended spell slots" (long rest phrasing)
- "Concentration" (capitalized when referencing the game mechanic)
- "you must maintain Concentration on this spell"
- "if your Concentration is broken"

---

## Damage

- Damage types are lowercase: fire, cold, necrotic, radiant, bludgeoning, piercing, slashing, psychic, poison, acid, lightning, thunder, force
- "takes [X] [type] damage" (not "suffers" or "receives")
- "damage rolls for this spell"
- "resistance to [type] damage" / "immunity to [type] damage"

---

## Timing

- "until the end of your next turn"
- "until the start of your next turn" — use carefully, these mean different things
- "for 1 minute" / "for 1 hour" / "until you finish a Long Rest"
- "Long Rest" and "Short Rest" are capitalized in 2024
- "at the end of each of its turns" (for ongoing effects on another creature)

---

## Targeting and Range

- "a creature of your choice that you can see within range"
- "one creature within 30 feet of you"
- "within [X] feet" (not "up to [X] feet away")
- "you touch a creature" (not "you touch one creature" in most spell contexts — check 2024 examples)

---

## Miscellaneous

- "you can't use this feature again until you finish a Long Rest" (recharge phrasing)
- "when you reach [X] level in this class" (subclass feature level phrasing)
- Ability score names capitalized: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma
- "your proficiency bonus" (lowercase)
- "add your proficiency bonus" / "double your proficiency bonus" (Expertise phrasing)
- "you are proficient with [weapon/tool/saving throw]"
